Peter F. Drucker

Peter Drucker getting a medal from George Bush

Peter Drucker is a considered to be the pioneer of management theory, he was a writer, teacher and consultant. His management ideology even though they were written more than 15 years ago are still relevant today. Peter Drucker's ideology influenced management practices in businesses, non-profits and governments. He has authored thirty-one books, which have been translated into more than twenty languages.

Peter Drucker was born in 1909 in Vienna and was educated there and in England. He worked as an economist for an international bank in London. After fleeing Hitler's Germany he took a job in London as a security analyst for an insurance firm.

After several teaching at different colleges, he embarked on a project, studying the management structure of General Motors. This led to his book the Concept of the Corporation, an immediate best seller in the United States and Japan.

Drucker was instrumental in the developement of America's first MBA program for working professionals at Claremont Graduate University.

He has been hailed as the seminal thinker, writer, and lecturer on contemporary organization. On June 21, 2002, Peter Drucker received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President George W. Bush.

Peter Drucker has recieved honorary doctorates from across the world. He passed away on November 11, 2005, at age ninety-five.
